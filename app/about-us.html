About Us

Zurich: Innovations Discovered by ipQuants

We Want to Quantify the World's Innovation Data

Having seen first-hand the value of innovation statistics in our roles advising and working for governments, universities and Fortune 500 companies, our vision is to make them widely accessible to others.

Our real-world experience shows that quantifying and expressing matters in statistics and numbers improves decision-making across a vast range of use cases. Policy making, finding the right development partner, identifying prospective customers and unearthing talent to fill open positions are just some of the applications.

We spent several years envisaging how to make the data accessible to all - not just domain experts. ipQuants is the result. Whether you’re a technology CTO, a freelance talent sourcer, an M&A pro or someone else who can benefit from quantified innovation statistics, we invite you to put ipQuants under the microscope.

Leadership

ipQuants’ founders have extensive commercial IP and innovation analytics and innovation economics know-how.

  • Tony Afram - Founder and CEO

    A native of Sweden, mechanical engineer, European patent attorney and ice hockey fan, Tony has spent his entire professional life in the world of invention and innovation.

    Through his career, he’s developed extensive commercial, legal and managerial expertise in the domain of global IP management. Tony has long incorporated innovation analytics into the advice and strategic plans he’s shared with executives, CTOs and corporate strategy leaders.

    He graduated from the Royal Institute of Technology (KTH) with an engineering degree.

  • Gianluca Tarasconi - Founder and Chief Data Officer

    Gianluca has spent his adult life working on business intelligence projects and creating data architectures for universities, government institutions and private companies across Europe and North America.

    He’s a frequently cited researcher in the innovation analytics and innovation economics fields, as well as an European Patent Office (EPO) honoree for his work improving the data quality of the EPO raw data product offering.

    Gianluca graduated with an engineering degree from Politecnico Milano.
